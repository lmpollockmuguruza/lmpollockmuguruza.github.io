Cinefilematic

For people who've already seen the obvious ones — and still scrolled the library for forty minutes and watched nothing.

It won't recommend you a film. It does something better: it interviews you properly — where you're arriving from, what tonight should do to you, how close to the bone it's allowed to cut — then composes a brief: a precise, faintly opinionated dossier. Hand it to the LLM of your choice and ask for five. What comes back is discovery, not dispensation: each film arrives with its provenance — where it came from, what shaped it — and the curator is even allowed to say that tonight, no film fits. Every brief is a numbered print — a different curator, a different house rule — so no two evenings come back the same. The taste stays yours; only the legwork is outsourced.

01 The occasion

Why tonight?

Be honest about the assignment. A nightcap, a date and a bout of insomnia all want very different films.

02 Arrival

Where are you arriving from?

Not what you want yet — what you're carrying in. Tap the map where tonight finds you: how much charge you have left, and how heavy or light the day sits.

This is the most useful answer on the page. Wanting a film to sit with you in a heavy mood is as legitimate as wanting one to lift you out — they lead to opposite shelves.

05 Anchors

The last one that really hit you?

Name a film you saw recently — loved, hated, couldn't shake. It tells the curator where your head is, and which way to push.

A precise miss teaches the curator more than a tenth love. Say why it missed if you can — too cold, too long, too pleased with itself.

08 Range

09 Coordinates

A few fixed stars. Free text reads best — be specific, be unguarded, name names.

10 The contract

How much rope?

You've drawn the picture. Now decide how literally the curator should take it — this is the biggest lever on what comes back.

Loose reads your overall mood and will surprise you — it's where the best picks tend to come from. Strict follows each answer closely, which is precise but can feel boxed-in. When in doubt, stay loose.

Only your genuine hard-nos. Over-avoiding strands the curator — if you'd merely prefer less of something, leave it unchecked and let the mood handle it.

01 The club

A season is programmed for a room, not a person. Describe yours honestly — including the ones who only come for the wine.
