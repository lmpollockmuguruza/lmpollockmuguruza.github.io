LUCAS POLLOCK MUGURUZA
A film questionnaire

Cinefilematic

For people who've already seen the obvious ones — and still scrolled the library for forty minutes and watched nothing.

It won't recommend you a film. It does something better: it interviews you for a few minutes, then composes a brief — a precise, faintly opinionated dossier on what you actually want tonight. Download it, hand it to the LLM of your choice, and ask for five. The taste stays yours; only the legwork is outsourced.

The programme

    Answer what you like; skip what you don't. The more you give it, the sharper the cut.

    01 The occasion

    Why tonight?

    Be honest about the assignment. A nightcap, a date and a bout of insomnia all want very different films.

    02 Mood & posture

    What do you want to feel?

    Pick one or two. There's no wrong answer — wanting to wallow is as valid as wanting to be lifted.

    Two clashing moods is fine — it tells the curator your range, not a contradiction.

    Hold me — easy to fall intoChallenge me — I'll lean in
    03 The anchor

    The last one that really hit you?

    Name a film you saw recently — loved, hated, couldn't shake. It tells the curator where your head is, and which way to push.

    04 The room

    Set the scene.

    The constraints the curator has to actually respect — no point recommending a three-hour fast for a restless table of four.

    Happy to read tonight
    05 Depth

    How deep do you swim?

    So the curator knows how far past the canon to reach — and never, ever condescends.

    06 Calibration

    Tick the ones you've already seen.

    A spread from the well-trodden to the deep cuts. It tells the curator your true range — and what to leave out.

    07 Coordinates

    Where's your taste pointed?

    A few fixed stars. Free text reads best — be specific, be unguarded, name names.

    08 Tonight's dials

    Fine-tune for this evening.

    Not who you are in general — where you want to sit tonight.

    Treat these as leanings, not targets. The centre keeps the field wide open; the extremes are where you box the curator in — so only push them when you mean it.

    09 Last thing

    How much rope?

    You've drawn the picture. Now decide how literally the curator should take it — this is the biggest lever on what comes back.

    Loose reads your overall mood and will surprise you — it's where the best picks tend to come from. Strict follows each answer closely, which is precise but can feel boxed-in. When in doubt, stay loose.

    Only your genuine hard-nos. Over-avoiding strands the curator — if you'd merely prefer less of something, leave it unchecked and let the mood handle it.

    Your brief is ready.

    Hand it to your LLM and ask for five. Or just say “go”.
