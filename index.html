About Me

I am an MSc Political Science and Political Economy student at the London School of Economics, with a predicted Distinction. My academic journey combines rigorous training in econometric methods, game theory, and critical analysis of contemporary political and economic challenges.

My research interests span electoral accountability, corruption, international development, and the intersection of technology with governance. Currently, I'm investigating how social networks, media structures, and socioeconomic factors shape the impact of information interventions on electoral accountability under the supervision of Prof Sarah Brierley.

With experience as a Policy Analyst at Broadpeak Advisory AG and Vice President of the Andalus Committee at LSE, I bring together academic rigor with practical policy insights. I've published research reaching over 50,000 readers on topics including migration, AI and inequality, and international development.
